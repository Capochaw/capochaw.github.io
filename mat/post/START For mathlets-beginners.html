# What is mathematics?

This is a personal appreciation. For me, math is the study of the description of a phenomena or called mathematical theory, which given some axioms (they are propositions that are true because are convenient), some signs and rules for those signs in function of the axioms, giving results (called theorems) using a chain of logic think.

This science is purely deductive. All the results that has been obtained, are from a chain of provable affirmations using previous knowledgement at difference of biology or medicine, which results are using testing/error scenarios and comparing the hypothesis and the obtained result.

It has a lot of social interaction and communication because the main idea is communicate the events that has been proved via theorems using the previous axioms from a mathematical theory. This allowed all the great amount of free knowledge we can have online from different resources online.

# Ok. So, how I start?

You will need to read books and lurk internet in general. The best source of knowledge are books because they were revised and analysed for errors and issues, trying to reduce the problems when you read about a topic.

Math books are not like a novel or similar literature. They are not pretended to read linearly. You can skip chapters, even the book if you learn what you needed or you were interested. They are written in a really simple format that you will memorize easily.

  • Preface: Will tell you the motivation that gave the author to write his work. Usually will tell you what the book will talk about.
  • Table of contents: selfexplained.
  • Chapters: The first chapters, probably the first, if not has made an explicit chapter called “prerequisites” or similar, will tell you the notation and facts that the book will use so you can understand what the author tries to explain you.
  • Appendix: Extra info that can be useful or not, depends on your interest on learning more or not about x or y topic on the book.
  • Index: This will be a main tool that if you do not remember some terminology defined or theorem that you will need to use for the part where you are reading, you can search there to not get loss and to refresh memory in general.

This is the basic core that you will need to know for learn any mathematical stuff by your own.

  • Basic Mathematics by Lang.
  • Calculus by Spivak.
  • Mathematical Analysis I and II by Zorich. (Use the first one as a complement of Spivak’s Calculus book)
  • Linear Algebra by Hoffman and Kunze.
  • Functions of One Complex Variable I by Conway.
  • Calculus on Manifolds by Spivak or Analysis on Manifolds by Munkres.
  • Differential Geometry of Curves and Surfaces by Tapp
  • Abstract Algebra by Dummit and Foote.
  • Topology by Munkres (only the first part of point set topology).

Obviously, some topics will need you another complementary topic, but you will be able to learn it searching in the web or looking at the bibliography that are at the end of the book.

This books will teach you about: * Analysis in Rn * Complex analysis * Linear Algebra in a field * Abstract Algebra (Algebraic Structures) * Differential Geometry * Point set Topology

With this core, you can understand other books that are more complex or abstract in nature.
